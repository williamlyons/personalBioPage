Early Life

I was born in Southborough a small town in Massachusetts. I have two brothers and a sister, all of whom are younger than I. I attended kindergarden at Finn school in my town. After kindergarden I spent eight years attending the Fay School, where I found that I had a passion for the sciences. During my eigthth grade science fair I created a small emp device out of capacitors, copper wiring, and a car battery. My teachers assumed that it wouldn't work, boy were they proved wrong when I flipped the switch and knocked three nearby computers out of action

High School

My father worked at a small boarding school in Southborough and through him I was granted a full scholarship I attended St. Marks School for four years. St. Marks took a holistic approach to education and consequently I was enrolled in a large variety of classes up until my senior year where i was able to specialize a little more. I enrolled in a STEM fellowship and began to study plant biology, specifically how the addition of certain phytohormones effected root growth in vetiver grass. My passion for bio-chemistry pushed me to apply ED to Washington University in St. Louis due to its stellar reputation in the sciences.

College

I was accepted into Washington University in 2013 and arrived in august 2014. I was initially enrolled in several sciences, such as general chemistry. I qucikly realized that I despised these large lecture courses and that a pursuing a major in these areas was not for me. Second semester I took computer science 131 and loved it. I loved being able to create things on my computer and was fascinated by the exploding field of big data. I am now enrolled in several computer science courses and I am attempting to get my BS in computer science
